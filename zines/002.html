THE 4 PLATES

The whole week runs on soy protein (PTS), eggs, chicken liver and a little powdered milk. Chicken breast is out — PTS + liver are in, for about R$60 less a week.

Meal 1 · Breakfast

SOY + EGG SCRAMBLE

ovos mexidos com PTS

  • 3 eggs (ovos)
  • 35 g dry PTS, rehydrated
  • onion + tomato + 1 tsp oil
  • garlic · salt · pepper
≈370 kcal 35 g protein

↳ Soak the PTS in hot water 10 min, squeeze dry, scramble right into the eggs. Soak in stock = way more flavour.

Meal 2 · Lunch

SOY "GROUND BEEF"

PTS temperada com legumes

  • 60 g dry PTS, rehydrated
  • 1 egg (to bind + protein)
  • tomato · onion · cabbage · carrot
  • 1 tsp oil · paprika · cumin
≈310 kcal 36 g protein

↳ Cook it exactly like carne moída — brown the onion, add drained PTS, tomato, simmer. Tastes like meat sauce, costs like nothing.

Meal 3 · Dinner

LIVER &amp; ONIONS

fígado de frango acebolado

  • 200 g chicken liver (fígado)
  • lots of onion + abobrinha / chuchu
  • 1 tsp oil · garlic · bay leaf
≈270 kcal 36 g protein

↳ Sear the liver hot & fast so it stays tender, pile on soft onions. Iron-packed, insanely cheap. Not into liver? → swap for coxa/sobrecoxa.

THE SWEET ONE ✦

Meal 4 · The Sweet One

BANANA-CINNAMON PUDIM

pudim proteico de banana

  • 2 eggs + ½ banana
  • 45 g powdered milk (leite em pó)
  • cinnamon (canela) + sweetener
≈360 kcal 25 g protein

↳ Blend, pour into cups, water-bath bake 180 graus ~25 min. Sets like a flan — cheap dessert, real protein, zero nuts.

THE SUNDAY BATCH

1

Soak all the PTS

Cover the full week's soy protein in hot water (or stock) for 10 min, then squeeze it dry. Do this first so it's ready.

2

Two PTS pans

One scramble base for breakfast, one 'ground beef' tomato base for lunch. Season them differently.

3

Sear the liver

Hot pan, quick sear, then a mountain of soft onions. Don't overcook it or it turns rubbery.

4

Bake the pudins + portion

Water-bath bake while everything cools. Portion 28, freeze flat, labelled by day.

FREEZE &amp; REHEAT

Cool first

Fully cool everything before freezing — the liver especially hates a slow refreeze.

Flat packs

Single portions, flat. PTS reheats perfectly; it soaked up all that sauce.

Thaw ahead

Fridge overnight, or microwave from frozen ~3 min. Liver: reheat gently, don't nuke it dry.

~1 month

Eat within a month. Pudins thaw sweetest in the fridge overnight.
